2025-2026

ADA ASISTENT | Current fav project

Visually impaired students are forced into linear learning. Lectures can't be skimmed. Structure isn't visible.

Finding one concept often means listening through hours of content. Much of the effort goes into organizing information, not learning it.

A friend and I built v0 of Ada at a hackathon to remove that overhead.

Ada is a complete learning system, not a chatbot. It analyzes lectures and documents, identifies their structure, and segments content automatically. Information is organized by meaning and connection, not folders and files. Students can jump directly to a few relevant minutes instead of replaying full lectures.

The system is built around how VI students actually learn. Navigation, structure, and feedback are fully customizable. Students shape the system around their own mental models instead of adapting to a fixed interface. Fully voice-navigable, with braille and mixed navigation options. No manual organization required -- Ada handles it in the background.

An AI layer runs continuously on top of this structure. It tracks student progress, adapts to how they learn, suggests reviews, and surfaces gaps at the right time. But it remains one part of a broader organizational system built for VI thinking patterns.

The hackathon demo led to invitations to present. We shared Ada with organizations for blind students and educators, gathered feedback, and iterated. That feedback continues to shape the system.

Ada is an evolving product, improving through collaboration with accessibility experts and researchers, built around the real needs of visually impaired students.

Sales Automation Platform | 2024:

Sales reps spent 20-30 minutes on each follow-up, pulling context from disconnected systems.

I built a system that brought everything together. Meeting data, emails, and CRM state unified into a single deal timeline. Relevant history surfaced automatically for each conversation. That context powered personalized follow-ups generated in seconds.

The system handled more than email. Detected risk across active deals. Suggested next steps. Coordinated outreach across channels in one workflow.

Follow-up time dropped by ~85-90%. The architecture later became the technical foundation for the client's startup.
